Project Workflow

This system predicts a user's blood group using retinal fundus and sclera images through machine learning. The process is completely non-invasive and follows the steps below:

  1. Image Acquisition: The user uploads retinal (fundus) and sclera images via the web interface.
  2. Preprocessing: Images are resized, normalized, and enhanced to remove noise and improve clarity.
  3. Feature Extraction: Important visual characteristics from both images are extracted.
  4. Feature Combination: Fundus and sclera features are merged into a single feature vector.
  5. Model Input: The combined features are fed into a trained Random Forest classifier.
  6. Prediction: The model outputs the most likely blood group.
  7. Evaluation: During training, the model’s accuracy, loss, and confusion matrix are recorded for analysis.
  8. Result Display: The predicted blood group is shown to the user along with extracted features.

Features Used for Prediction

The system uses a total of 10 extracted features from the fundus and sclera images, plus user metadata, to predict blood group.

1. Fundus Vessel Density

Measures the proportion of blood vessel pixels in the retinal image, indicating vascular richness.

2. Fundus Tortuosity

Quantifies the curvature of retinal blood vessels, which can vary between individuals.

3. Optic Disc Diameter

Size of the optic nerve head in the fundus image, related to eye structure and health.

4. Macula Brightness

Average pixel intensity in the macula region, indicating pigment and light reflection properties.

5. Retinal Ridge Count

Counts ridge-like patterns in the retina, related to vascular branching complexity.

6. Sclera Redness Index

Measures the level of redness in the sclera, often caused by blood vessel visibility.

7. Sclera Texture Contrast

Determines the variation in brightness in the sclera, indicating blood vessel prominence.

8. Sclera Blue-Yellow Ratio

Analyzes color balance in the sclera, influenced by pigmentation and oxygenation.

9. Age

User’s age in years, as blood group feature expression can vary slightly across age groups.

10. Body Mass Index (BMI)

Calculated from height and weight; certain physiological patterns may influence prediction.
